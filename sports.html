Sports Around the World
Popular Sports in North America Popular Sports in South America Popular Sports in Asia

Basketball is one of the most popular sports in the Northern
Hemisphere. Its popularity is epitomized by the National Basketball
Association, the most recognized professional league in the world.
It features players from around the world and has attracted viewers
from hundreds of countries.

Also known as football, soccer is played on a 110 meter
pitch, and features close to 250 million players representing 200 countries. Arguably,
the most popular sporting event in the world is the quadrennial FIFA World Cup. In the
summer of 2014, 5 million people flocked to Brazil to watch one of the 64 matches, in
addition millions watching at home around the world. Teams and players receive national
fame for participation; the country that took home the most glory was Germany, defeating
Argentina 1-0 in the championship match.

Cricket is a bat and ball game between teams of 11 players.
Cricket is mainly popular in the Asian and European countries; the
height of its popularity is on the Indian side. There are many leagues
around the world including the Indian Premier League.

American Football originated from European rugby. It is a
contact sport played on a 100 yard field. The National Football League
is the professional equivalent of the NBA for Football, and
features 32 teams representing various regions of the United States

The name "rugby" comes from the Rugby School where it was developed. The sport features an oblong ball, which one must carry to an endzone. Any measure of kicking, throwing, punching, can be used to move the ball forward. Often, the game turns to be very violent.

Similar to tennis, badminton is a racquet sport that can be played as singles or a team of two. The sport is played indoors and features a smaller court size than tennis. The number one ranked male and female is from India; India also took home the bronze medal in Women's Singles in the London 2012 Summer Olympics.

Referred to as America's past time, Baseball has been a popular sport since its development in the early 1900's. Similar to cricket, baseball is a bat and ball game played on a mound; a pitch featuring a fan-shaped field. The preeminent league for professional baseball is the MLB, which features teams based in the United States and Canada.

Polo is a team sport played on horseback. Players score by driving a small white plastic or wooden ball into the opposing team's goal using a long- handled mallet. The game originated in Persia, circa 6 century BC, and spread to Turkey and slowly to South America.

Also referred to as ping pong, table tennis is a racquet sport that originated in England. Players hit a lightweight ball across a small table, rallying to win the point by an opponent's error of hitting the ball out, or into the net. The current number one ranked player is Ma Long from China, an acclaimed tennis player who has received many awards in the sport.
